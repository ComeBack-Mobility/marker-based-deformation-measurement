# Deformation Measurement Protocol

Interfragmentary micromovements were measured photogrammetrically from sequential images of surface markers using custom image-analysis scripts. For each specimen and loading configuration, images acquired at predefined loading steps were arranged into ordered loading series. For bending experiments, images were additionally aligned before marker-based measurements were calculated. Alignment was performed as rigid in-plane registration, allowing translation and rotation only, and was used to reduce apparent marker displacement caused by small changes in specimen position between images. Alignment quality was assessed using the enhanced correlation coefficient (ECC), and only registrations with ECC values above 0.99 were accepted for high-confidence analysis.

To convert image measurements into physical units, a caliper with a known opening was placed in the image plane in the first photograph. The distance between the caliper edges was measured in pixels and divided by the known physical distance to obtain the calibration value.

Marker candidates were first detected automatically in the first photograph based on segmentation of green circles in the marker centers. This provided approximate marker locations. The center positions of the selected markers were then refined using the marker vertical and horizontal lines around each candidate to estimate the true marker center more accurately. This refinement step reduced localization error and improved the consistency of subsequent displacement measurements.

After marker localization, relative differences between predefined marker pairs were calculated for each image and expressed in millimeters along the image axes. In this repository, the X component corresponds to deformation normal to the load direction and the Y component corresponds to deformation in the load direction. Experimental deformations were recalculated from signed marker-pair distance components using the unloaded image from loading series 1 as the common reference state. The final marker-pair deformation columns were then shifted so that the mean first loaded step across trials 1-3 was zero. Trial 0 was retained in per-image output tables for transparency and quality control, but it was excluded from mean deformation and 95% CI calculations. Trial 0 was a stabilizational loading series performed to remove unnecessary gaps between loading interfaces; only the first photographs from this series were used for calibration purposes.

For interpretation, marker pairs were assigned to Whole-bone or Fracture zone groups. In the Bone 2 example, Whole-bone pairs were 2->6 in bending in sagittal plane and 4->7 in compression, whereas Fracture zone pairs were 3->5 and 6->1.


Mean deformation and confidence intervals are calculated by the repository statistics command from `Deformation_X_mm` and `Deformation_Y_mm`. Trials 1-3 are pooled at each load step, and 95% confidence intervals are calculated using the Student t distribution.
